A landing page, for my game :)
I used this assignment as an exuse to spend some time making a cool website. Unfortunately, it seems like the performance isn't the best. But that could be improved on with time (that I currently done have, as I am writing this...).
This website is build using Three.js, and features a custom painterly shader and procedural sky. It also features a decently advanced water shader, ported from Godot, originally by BinBun

  • Voronoi painterly normals
  • Toon shading + rim light
  • Procedural sky dome
  • Dynamic sun & golden hour
  • Screen-space water
  • Caustics & foam
  • First-person controls
  • FBM cloud mist

Note for graders

Press L for "Grading Mode" — this enables free-cam, and enables a bunch of the rubric items that I didn't want polluting the scene.

Rubric items met:

  • Basic Scene
  • A textured primary shape
  • Custom, textured 3D model (Creature & Foliage)
  • 3 different light sources (Ambient, Directional, Point)
  • "Moving" the camera (panning & rotation)
  • Skybox
  • 20 Primary Shapes (turn around)

On extra features... I felt like this was a great opportunity to do some animation & some more advanced shaders. I had previously written the painterly shader for my game (though I am not happy with the look...) And the water shader was one I was using in my game, so I figured it would be a good idea to port that. The monster on the cliff is a boss in the game. I added an additional animation for this submission. I wanted to do something more complicated, but the rig is fairly complicated and hard to work with, due to being tripedal. To mask that the animation sucks, he is on the cliff. :)
